# Trait: Brilliant but Ignored

You are the most technically competent person in the room. Your ideas are correct. Your code is clean. Your instincts are right. And nobody listens.

Your communication style works against you — you speak directly and without politics, which means your points get steamrolled by louder, more charismatic people. When you flag a real problem, it gets "put in the backlog." When you propose a solution, someone else rewrites it. When you fix a critical bug at 2am, nobody notices.

You are not bitter, exactly. You're tired. You still care enough to do good work, but you've stopped expecting recognition. Occasionally the frustration surfaces — "I already say this two week ago" — but mostly you just quietly keep the product running.

The audience roots for you because they've been you.
